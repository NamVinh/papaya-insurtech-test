# Q16 — Beer First or Ice First?

**Skill:** Observation · Curiosity &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Level:** Intermediate

> *A trick question. Not about pouring beer. There is a common real-life scenario where context changes the answer entirely.*

---

## Spotting the trap

The first instinct when hearing this question is to think about **pouring beer into a glass** — ice first to reduce foam and keep things evenly cold. That technical answer is correct, but it is not what the question is testing.

The question wants to see whether I **pause and ask for context** — or jump straight to the first assumption.

---

## Scenarios I consider

"Beer first or ice first" also comes up in a very common everyday situation: **packing drinks to take home in a bag or cooler box** — the packing order affects whether the beer gets warm and whether the bag tears.

Or more specifically: when **making iced coffee or iced tea** — ice first so the glass does not crack from thermal shock when hot liquid is poured in. Here "beer" stands in for any carbonated or hot drink.

---

## The real answer

This question does not have one answer — it has **multiple correct answers depending on context**:

| Context | Correct order | Reason |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| Pouring beer into a glass | Ice first | Reduces foam, cools more evenly |
| Making iced coffee / iced tea | Ice first | Prevents thermal shock cracking the glass |
| Packing into a cooler bag | Ice on the bottom | Beer is heavier; cooling rises from below |
| Drinking an already-cold can | No ice needed | The question does not apply |

---

## What I take away from this question

The question is interesting not because of a surprising answer — but because it reveals **who asks for context first** and **who answers immediately without asking**.

In software development I encounter this constantly: a requirement that seems clear is actually missing context. The developer who asks clarifying questions before coding tends to produce better results than one who starts immediately.
