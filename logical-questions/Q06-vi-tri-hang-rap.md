# Q6 — Best Position in the Cinema Queue

**Skill:** Observation &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Level:** Intermediate

> *Front, middle, or back of the queue? "Best" by what criteria?*

---

## First: what does "best" mean?

The question has no answer until the goal is defined. I take "best" to mean: **get to your seat the fastest, with the least energy spent waiting**.

---

## Where is the real bottleneck?

Most people assume a long queue means a long wait. But the real bottleneck is the **ticket scanner** — not the length of the queue.

- If the cinema has 2 scanners: stand at the front of whichever lane is moving faster.
- If there is only 1 scanner: being at the front is clearly advantageous.
- In practice the queue has already formed by the time you arrive — you rarely get to choose "the front."

---

## Analysing each position

| Position | Reality | Trade-off |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Front** | Enter first, best seat choice | Must arrive early, longest standing wait |
| **Middle** | Balanced — not too early, not too late | Less control |
| **Back** | Can arrive later, less standing | Enter last, fewer seat options |

---

## Conclusion

**The best position depends on whether you value seat quality or waiting time more.**

Personally I choose **middle** in most cases — no need to arrive very early, and seat choice is not a concern since I pre-book assigned seats online. If seats are not pre-assigned, I aim for **the front** by arriving 15 minutes early.

> A second-order effect that is often ignored: the group **immediately behind me** also affects my throughput — a large group just ahead can slow the whole queue even if I am "in the middle."
